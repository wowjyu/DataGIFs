Data GIFs
Shaping Data Stories with Animated Charts and Short Loops

Xinhuan Shu,  Aoyu Wu,  Leni Yang, and Huamin Qu

Data GIFs Collection Analysis Interviews

Data GIFs Collection

To better understand data GIFs in current practices, we collected a list of data GIFs from diverse sources. The selection criteria were twofold: 1) data GIFs should contain visualization and convey insights, excluding system demonstrations; 2) the collection should capture a range of stories and designs.

All data GIFs we collected can be found in the gallery.

Analysis

To explore the design space, we considered the innate nature of GIFs, namely, the temporal context and the loop. We coded all data GIFs based on: 1) what the GIF timeline encodes, 2) what visualization they use, 3) how they expose context between frames, 4) how they circulate the loop.

We summarized results in the figure. Each row indicates a combination of certain designs (Timeline x Exposure x Loop). The numbers represents the amounts of data GIFs with this type of visualization in our corpus. The analysis of each data GIF can be found in the online version.

Interviews

The goal is to capture the perceptions of data GIFs from the perspective of viewers and derive design implications. We interviewed 12 participants with varying levels of visualization literacy from different fields, including visualization (P1-P5), graphic designs (P6-P8), economics (P9-P10), and engineering (P11-P12).

Study Design:
To keep participants motivated and engaged, we selected 8 representative data GIFs from our corpus. We asked participants first to watch GIFs until they could recognize the underlying data insights. They then reported their findings and shared thoughts about designs and reading experiences. The interview was recorded and lasted about 35 minutes.
